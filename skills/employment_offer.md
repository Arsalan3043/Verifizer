# skill: employment_offer.md
# Domain knowledge for employment offer letter verification
# Used by verification_agent to identify misleading claims

## Purpose
Verify claims made by HR, recruiters, or hiring managers
against the actual offer letter, appointment letter, or employment contract.
Applies to full-time, part-time, and contractual employment offers.

---

## Red Flags — Claims That Are Almost Always Misleading

- **"CTC and in-hand are almost the same"** → Check CTC breakup. Employer PF contribution, gratuity, insurance premiums, and other components reduce in-hand significantly.
- **"Notice period is flexible"** → Check notice period clause exactly. Verbal flexibility means nothing if the document says 3 months.
- **"Bonus is guaranteed every year"** → Check bonus clause. Most bonuses are "at company's discretion" or linked to performance targets.
- **"You will be confirmed after 3 months"** → Check probation period clause. Probation can be extended and termination during probation may require no notice.
- **"Variable pay will always be 100%"** → Check variable pay conditions. Variables are linked to performance ratings which are subjective.
- **"Joining bonus is yours to keep"** → Check joining bonus clawback clause. Often must be returned if you leave within 1-2 years.
- **"We will match your current benefits"** → Check exact benefits listed — health insurance sum insured, number of dependents covered, leave policy.
- **"Work from home is permanent"** → Check if WFH policy is in the offer letter or is just a current informal practice.
- **"You can take leave whenever"** → Check leave policy — number of days, carry-forward rules, encashment policy.
- **"Non-compete clause is just a formality"** → Non-compete clauses can restrict where you work next. Check duration and scope.

## Critical Clauses to Always Extract from Document

- **CTC breakup** — fixed pay, variable pay, allowances, employer contributions, benefits
- **In-hand / take-home salary** — after all deductions including PF, professional tax, TDS
- **Probation period** — duration, conditions for confirmation, termination rights during probation
- **Notice period** — from employee side and employer side (they may differ)
- **Joining bonus** — amount, conditions, clawback period if leaving early
- **Variable / performance pay** — percentage of CTC, how it is calculated, when it is paid
- **Annual bonus / increment policy** — guaranteed or discretionary, frequency
- **Leave policy** — earned leave, sick leave, casual leave, carry-forward, encashment
- **Health insurance** — sum insured, number of dependents, waiting periods, type of policy
- **Non-compete clause** — duration, geography, which companies or roles are restricted
- **Non-solicitation clause** — restrictions on poaching colleagues or clients after leaving
- **Intellectual property clause** — who owns work created during employment
- **Confidentiality / NDA** — scope and duration
- **Termination clause** — grounds for termination without notice (misconduct, etc.)
- **Role and designation** — exact title, reporting structure, job description reference

## Common Misleading Statements by HR / Recruiters

| What HR Says | What to Actually Check |
|---|---|
| "CTC is ₹X lakhs" | Break down into fixed, variable, and non-cash components |
| "In-hand will be around ₹Y" | Calculate yourself: Fixed - PF (employee) - PT - TDS |
| "Appraisals are every 6 months" | Check if appraisal cycle is in writing or just verbal policy |
| "We have great work-life balance" | Check leave policy and whether leave encashment is available |
| "Non-compete is standard, nobody enforces it" | Enforceability varies but risk exists — check scope carefully |
| "You can negotiate notice period when you resign" | Notice period in offer letter is what HR/legal will enforce |
| "Joining bonus is fully yours after 6 months" | Read exact clawback clause — may be 12 or 18 months |
| "Variable pay is usually achieved by everyone" | Check what happens if company misses targets — variable may be zero |

## Consumer Rights — India (Employment Laws)

- **Offer Letter is a Contract**: A signed offer letter is legally binding on both employer and employee
- **PF Deduction**: Mandatory for salary below ₹15,000/month basic — employer must contribute equal amount
- **Gratuity**: Payable after 5 years of continuous service — 15 days salary per year of service
- **Notice Period Enforcement**: Company can enforce notice period or demand payment in lieu
- **Non-Compete Enforceability**: Indian courts have generally not enforced post-employment non-competes strictly — but case-by-case
- **Maternity Benefit Act**: 26 weeks paid maternity leave for first two children — must be in writing
- **Sexual Harassment Policy (POSH)**: Company must have an Internal Complaints Committee — ask for policy
- **Termination During Probation**: Company may terminate with shorter or no notice — check clause carefully
- **TDS on Salary**: Employer must deduct TDS and provide Form 16 at end of financial year
- **Full and Final Settlement**: Must be completed within 30-45 days of last working day

## Known High-Risk Clauses Often Downplayed

- Garden leave clause — company can put you on leave during notice period and restrict you from joining competitor
- Broad IP assignment clauses that claim ownership of personal projects created outside work hours
- Moonlighting prohibition clauses — some companies prohibit any outside work or freelancing
- Relocation clauses — company can transfer you to any location in India without additional compensation
- Clawback on stock options — unvested options are forfeited immediately on resignation
- Arbitration clauses — disputes must go to arbitration, not civil court (limits your options)
- At-will termination clauses disguised in probation extension language
- Discretionary increment clauses — company has sole right to decide salary revision quantum
