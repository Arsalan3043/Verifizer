# skill: rent_agreement.md
# Domain knowledge for rent agreement verification
# Used by verification_agent to identify misleading claims

## Purpose
Verify claims made by landlords, brokers, or property agents
against the actual rent agreement / lease deed.
Applies to residential and commercial rental agreements.

---

## Red Flags — Claims That Are Almost Always Misleading

- **"You can leave anytime"** → Check the lock-in period clause. Most agreements have a 6-12 month lock-in where tenant cannot vacate without penalty.
- **"Security deposit is fully refundable"** → Check deduction clauses. Landlords can deduct for damages, unpaid utilities, repainting, cleaning.
- **"Rent won't increase for X years"** → Check rent escalation clause. Many agreements have automatic 5-10% annual increase built in.
- **"All maintenance is the landlord's responsibility"** → Check maintenance clause. Minor repairs are often the tenant's responsibility.
- **"Society amenities are free"** → Check if society maintenance charges are included in rent or separate.
- **"You can sublet if needed"** → Check subletting clause. Most agreements prohibit subletting without written landlord permission.
- **"Notice period is just one month"** → Check notice period clause for both tenant and landlord — they may differ.
- **"No need to register the agreement"** → Agreements above 11 months must be registered in India. Unregistered agreements above 11 months are not legally enforceable.
- **"Pets are allowed"** → Check pet policy clause explicitly. Verbal permission means nothing if the agreement says no pets.
- **"You can do work from home / run a business"** → Check use of premises clause. Residential agreements often prohibit commercial activity.

---

## Critical Clauses to Always Extract from Document

- **Lock-in period** — minimum period tenant must stay, penalty for early exit
- **Notice period** — how much notice tenant and landlord must give each other
- **Security deposit amount** — and conditions for refund / deductions
- **Rent escalation clause** — whether rent increases annually and by how much
- **Maintenance responsibilities** — who pays for what repairs
- **Society maintenance charges** — included in rent or paid separately
- **Utility bills** — water, electricity, gas — who pays
- **Subletting clause** — whether subletting is permitted
- **Use of premises** — residential only, or commercial activity allowed
- **Pet policy** — allowed or prohibited
- **Termination clause** — conditions under which landlord can terminate
- **Renewal terms** — how and when agreement can be renewed
- **Dispute resolution** — jurisdiction for legal disputes

## Common Misleading Phrases Used by Landlords/Brokers

| What Is Said | What to Actually Check |
|---|---|
| "It is just a formality" | Read every clause — formalities become legally binding |
| "We can sort it out verbally" | Only written clauses in registered agreement are enforceable |
| "Security deposit will be returned in full" | Check deduction list and timeline for return |
| "Society is very cooperative" | Check if society has restrictions on tenants (bachelors, non-veg, etc.) |
| "Parking is included" | Check if parking is mentioned and which spot is allotted |
| "Landlord will not disturb you" | Check landlord's right to inspect the property with notice |
| "Agreement is just 11 months to save registration fees" | 11-month agreements are common but offer you less legal protection |

## Consumer Rights — India (Tenancy Laws)

- **Security Deposit Limit**: Most state Rent Control Acts cap security deposit at 2-3 months rent (varies by state)
- **Registration**: Agreements above 11 months must be registered under Registration Act, 1908 — protects tenant's rights
- **Stamp Duty**: Required for valid agreement — varies by state (0.25% to 5% of total rent value)
- **Rent Control Acts**: Many states have rent control acts protecting tenants from arbitrary eviction and excessive rent increase
- **Notice for Eviction**: Landlord must give proper legal notice before eviction — verbal requests are not valid
- **Deposit Return Timeline**: Should be returned within 15-30 days of vacating after deductions (check state law)
- **Receipt for Rent**: Tenant has the right to demand rent receipts for every payment made

## Known High-Risk Clauses Often Hidden in Agreements

- Automatic renewal clauses that lock you in for another term if you don't give notice exactly on time
- Clauses allowing landlord to enter property without notice
- Penalty clauses for late rent that compound daily
- Clauses making tenant responsible for all repairs regardless of cause
- Society NOC requirements that can block you from registering the agreement
- Force majeure clauses that release landlord from obligations
- Clauses that make tenant liable for structural damage caused by natural wear
- Clauses that waive tenant's right to contest eviction legally
